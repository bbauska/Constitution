Capitalism

Economic system based on private ownership of the means of production and market freedom.

Communism

Movement and political system, developed since the 19th century, based on class struggle and the suppression of private property of the means of production.

Conservative

Tendency to preserve established values and principles.

Anarchy

Doctrine that advocates the suppression of the State and governmental power in defense of the absolute freedom of the individual.

Fascim

Political and social movement of a totalitarian nature that developed in Italy in the first half of the 20th century, and was characterized by corporatism and nationalist exaltation.

Nationalism

Fervent feeling of belonging to a nation and identification with its reality and its history.

Separatism

Political trend that advocates the separation of a territory from the state to which it belongs, to achieve its independence or integrate into another country.

NationalSocialism

Political and social movement of the German Third Reich, totalitarian, pan-German and racist in nature.

Socialism

System of social and economic organization based on the collective or state ownership and administration of the means of production and distribution of goods.

SocialDemocracy

Political movement that advocates democratic and reformist socialism.

Liberalism

Political doctrine that postulates individual and social freedom in politics and private initiative in economic and cultural matters, limiting the intervention of the State and public powers in these areas.

Totalitarian

Doctrine and political regimes, developed during the 20th century, in which the State concentrates all powers in a single party and coercively controls social relations under a single official ideology.

Traditionalism

Political system that consists of maintaining or reestablishing ancient institutions, in adhering to the ideas, norms or customs of the past.

Centrism

Political trend or group whose ideology is intermediate between the right and the left.
